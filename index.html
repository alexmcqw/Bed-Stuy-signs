Abstract

This research project examines global sales patterns across multiple geographic regions and product categories during the 2024 fiscal year. Through comprehensive data analysis and visualization techniques, we investigate revenue distribution, market penetration, and performance metrics to identify key trends and growth opportunities.

Our study encompasses 5,234 transactions across five major geographic regions, analyzing both quantitative metrics and qualitative performance indicators. The methodology combines statistical analysis with interactive data visualization to present findings in an accessible and interpretable format.

Research Objectives

  • Analyze revenue trends and growth patterns across global markets
  • Compare regional performance against established targets and benchmarks
  • Identify correlations between product categories and market success
  • Evaluate key performance indicators and their impact on business outcomes

Methodology

This research employs machine learning techniques to classify storefront signage typography, enabling quantitative analysis of urban change in Bedford-Stuyvesant, Brooklyn.

Data Collection

Storefront images were collected through systematic street-level photography across the study area. Images were captured at consistent angles and lighting conditions to ensure comparability.

Classification Model

Using Google's Teachable Machine platform, we trained a binary classification model to distinguish between traditional and modern typography styles. The model was trained on a curated dataset of 1,000+ labeled images.

Analysis Framework

Predictions were generated for thousands of storefront images, with confidence thresholds applied to ensure data quality. Results were geocoded and temporally indexed to enable spatial and temporal analysis.

Research Context

Bedford-Stuyvesant has experienced significant demographic and economic changes over the past two decades. This project examines how storefront signage—a visible marker of neighborhood character—reflects and potentially signals these transformations.

Gentrification Indicators

Typography style serves as a proxy for business type and target demographic. Traditional signage often correlates with long-established, community-serving businesses, while modern typography frequently appears alongside newer, higher-end establishments.
